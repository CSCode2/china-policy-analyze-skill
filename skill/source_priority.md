# Source Priority Hierarchy

## Domestic Policy Sources

### S-Level (Supreme Authority)

These are the highest-authority sources in the Chinese policy system. They set the direction that all lower-level documents must follow.

**Included sources:**
- Central Committee of the Communist Party of China documents (中共中央文件)
- Five-Year Plans for National Economic and Social Development (国民经济和社会发展五年规划)
- Government Work Reports (政府工作报告), delivered at the annual NPC session
- Central Economic Work Conference conclusions (中央经济工作会议)
- Xi Jinping's important speeches and articles (习近平总书记重要讲话和文章)
- National laws enacted by the National People's Congress or its Standing Committee (全国人大及其常委会法律)
- Administrative regulations issued by the State Council (国务院行政法规)

**When to use:**
- Every policy analysis must start with S-level sources if available
- Any claim about central policy direction must be grounded in S-level evidence
- S-level sources override all other levels when conflicts arise

**Citation requirements:**
- Full document title
- Date of issuance or delivery
- Specific passage or section reference
- Whether the source is a full-text document, a summary, or a news report of a speech

---

### A-Level (Central Implementation Authority)

These are the central-level implementation bodies and official information channels. They translate S-level direction into specific measures and provide authoritative data.

**Included sources:**
- State Council documents (国务院文件), including State Council opinions, notices, and measures
- National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC / 国家发改委)
- Ministry of Finance (MOF / 财政部)
- People's Bank of China (PBOC / 中国人民银行)
- National Bureau of Statistics (NBS / 国家统计局)
- Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT / 工信部)
- Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST / 科技部)
- Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM / 商务部)
- National Financial Regulatory Administration (NFRA / 国家金融监督管理总局)
- China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC / 中国证监会)
- State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR / 市场监管总局)
- Ministry of Public Security (MPS / 公安部)
- Ministry of Justice (MOJ / 司法部)
- Supreme People's Court (最高人民法院) — judicial interpretations, guiding cases, typical cases
- Supreme People's Procuratorate (最高人民检察院) — procuratorial guidance, typical cases
- People's Daily (人民日报) — for leadership speech full text and editorial board articles
- Xinhua News Agency (新华社) — for leadership speech full text and official releases
- Qiushi (求是) — for Xi Jinping's articles and theoretical pieces
- People.cn speech database (人民网领导人活动报道集) — for comprehensive speech records

**When to use:**
- For specific implementation measures, funding, pilots, and deadlines
- For macro data and financial conditions
- For judicial and enforcement signals
- For authoritative speech texts not available elsewhere

**Citation requirements:**
- Issuing ministry or institution
- Document number if available
- Date of issuance
- Specific section or paragraph reference

---

### B-Level (Provincial Authority)

These are provincial-level governments and their departments, plus major city governments. They implement central policy with local adaptation.

**Included sources:**
- Provincial people's governments (省级人民政府)
- Provincial development and reform commissions (省级发改委)
- Provincial finance departments (省级财政厅)
- Provincial industry and information technology departments (省级工信厅)
- Provincial science and technology departments (省级科技厅)
- Provincial commerce departments (省级商务厅)
- Provincial market regulation bureaus (省级市场监管局)
- Provincial courts (高级人民法院) — judgments and guidance
- Provincial procuratorates (省级检察院) — guidance and typical cases
- Provincial public security departments (省级公安厅)
- Governments of directly-administered municipalities (直辖市) and sub-provincial cities (副省级城市)

**When to use:**
- For local implementation comparison
- For provincial-level funding, pilots, and project information
- For regional enforcement trends

**Citation requirements:**
- Province name
- Issuing department
- Document number if available
- Date
- Specific measure or wording

---

### C-Level (Local Implementation Evidence)

These are the most granular implementation signals. They show whether policy is actually being executed with resources, not just rhetoric.

**Included sources:**
- Municipal departments (市级部门)
- Industrial parks and zones (产业园区, 开发区)
- High-tech zones (高新区)
- Project application notices (项目申报通知)
- Local procurement and tender information (采购和招标信息)
- District-level implementation notices (区级实施通知)
- Park-level preferential policy documents (园区优惠政策文件)

**When to use:**
- For verifying that central/provincial policy has reached the implementation level
- For identifying concrete opportunities (funding, project applications, procurement)
- For confirming that pilots are actually operational

**Citation requirements:**
- Municipality, district, or park name
- Issuing department or management committee
- Notice or tender number if available
- Date
- Specific funding amount, deadline, or eligibility criteria

---

### D-Level (Official Interpretation)

These are secondary but official explanations of policy. They clarify intent but do not create new policy.

**Included sources:**
- Official interpretation articles (官方解读文章), typically published on gov.cn or ministry websites
- Press conference transcripts (新闻发布会文字实录)
- Ministry spokesperson statements
- Expert explanations commissioned or endorsed by the issuing body
- Legislative intent explanations (立法说明)
- Policy briefing materials for NPC/CPPCC sessions

**When to use:**
- To clarify ambiguous wording in higher-level documents
- To understand legislative or policy intent
- To confirm which interpretation the issuing body intended

**Limitations:**
- D-level sources clarify existing policy; they do not create new policy
- If a D-level interpretation contradicts an S/A-level text, the text prevails
- Always note who provided the interpretation and in what context

---

### E-Level (Informational Lead Only)

These are commercial and non-official sources. They may provide leads, tips, or context but must never be used as primary evidence for policy claims.

**Included sources:**
- Commercial media (财经媒体): Caixin, 21st Century Business Herald, Economic Observer, Securities Daily, etc.
- Self-media (自媒体): WeChat public accounts, Weibo accounts,知乎 columns, industry newsletters
- Market commentary: Analyst reports, think-tank briefings, consultant publications
- Industry association reports (unless government-commissioned)
- Foreign media reports on Chinese policy

**When to use:**
- As leads to identify documents or events to verify with official sources
- For market reaction and sentiment context
- For identifying emerging topics that have not yet been covered by official channels

**Strict limitations:**
- Never cite E-level sources as evidence for what official policy is or says
- Never quote E-level paraphrases as if they were original wording
- If an E-level source identifies a policy development, verify it with an S/A/B/C-level source before citing it
- E-level sources about policy intent, infighting, or behind-the-scenes dynamics are never reliable as evidence

---

## Foreign Policy Source Priorities

When analyzing foreign policy, geopolitical risk, or international economic policy that affects China:

### FP-S Level (Supreme Authority for Foreign Policy)
- Central Foreign Affairs Work Conference conclusions (中央外事工作会议)
- Xi Jinping's speeches on international relations and foreign policy
- Central Committee documents on foreign affairs and national security
- National security laws and regulations

### FP-A Level (Central Foreign Policy Implementation)
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA / 外交部): statements, spokesperson remarks, position papers
- Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM / 商务部): trade policy, sanctions responses, trade remedy notices
- National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC): foreign investment catalogs, outbound investment guidance
- Central Foreign Affairs Commission documents
- National Defense-related policy documents (when publicly available)

### FP-B Level (Multilateral and Bilateral Frameworks)
- Official texts of bilateral treaties, agreements, and joint statements
- China's submitted documents to WTO, UN, and other international organizations
- Free trade agreement texts and implementation rules
- Belt and Road official cooperation documents

### FP-C Level (Foreign Policy Implementation at Operational Level)
- Customs (GACC / 海关总署): import/export regulations, HS code changes, enforcement notices
- State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE / 外汇管理局): foreign exchange policy, cross-border payment rules
- CIQ (entry-exit inspection and quarantine) notices
- Port and special zone policy documents

### FP-D Level (Official Foreign Policy Interpretation)
- MFA press conference transcripts
- MOFCOM press conference transcripts
- Embassy and consulate official statements
- Official white papers on foreign policy topics

### FP-E Level (External Analysis Only)
- Foreign government reports (e.g., USTR reports, EU trade policy reviews)
- International organization reports (e.g., IMF Article IV, WTO trade policy reviews)
- Foreign think-tank analysis
- International media reports
- Commercial geopolitical risk assessments

**Strict limitations for FP-E:**
- These sources reflect external perspectives, not Chinese official positions
- They may provide useful data and analysis but must never be cited as evidence of Chinese policy intent
- Always pair FP-E sources with S/A/Fp-A/Fp-B level sources when discussing Chinese positions

---

## Cross-Domain Source Integration

When a question involves both domestic and foreign policy (e.g., sanctions impact, export controls, foreign investment):

1. Start with the highest level domestic source for the domestic policy dimension
2. Start with the highest level foreign policy source for the foreign policy dimension
3. Integrate at the overlap point (e.g., MOFCOM documents that implement both domestic and foreign policy)
4. Never use a foreign-only source to make claims about Chinese domestic policy, or vice versa
5. When external and Chinese sources conflict on factual matters (e.g., sanctions scope), present both and note the conflict
